Smart City Project: Business Cases

Optimal Road Network Design

Goal: Efficient connectivity for the movement of people and goods in Aurangabad (1500 sq. km).

SDG Goal: 11 | Target: 11.2 | Indicator: 11.2.1

Algorithms Used:

  • Merge Sort: Sorting algorithm for road data. Application: Sorting road network data for better decision-making.
  • Quick Sort: Sorting algorithm for route prioritization. Application: Used to sort route options for optimization.
  • Dijkstra's Algorithm: Computes the shortest path to minimize travel costs. Application: Used to find the shortest path between critical locations.
  • Floyd-Warshall Algorithm: Computes all-pairs shortest paths. Application: Used to analyze the overall network for all routes.
  • Prim’s Algorithm: Finds the minimum spanning tree. Application: Used to connect essential nodes with minimal construction cost.
  • Kruskal’s Algorithm: Another approach for finding the minimum spanning tree. Application: Ensures minimal cost in connecting essential areas.

Smart Public Transport Scheduling

Goal: Optimize public transportation schedules for better commuter convenience.

SDG Goal: 11 | Target: 11.2 | Indicator: 11.2.1

Algorithms Used:

  • Bubble Sort: Sorting algorithm for small datasets. Application: Sorting transport schedules or route options.
  • Insertion Sort: Sorting algorithm for optimizing bus schedules. Application: Sorting small datasets of bus schedules.
  • Bellman-Ford Algorithm: Computes shortest paths considering negative weights. Application: Helps in planning efficient bus routes.
  • Greedy Algorithm for Activity Selection: Selects maximum non-overlapping activities. Application: Ensures buses do not overlap.
  • Genetic Algorithm: Optimization based on natural selection. Application: Used for optimizing routes and schedules.
  • Simulated Annealing: Probabilistic optimization. Application: Used to minimize waiting times and balance loads.

Water and Waste Management System

Goal: Ensure efficient resource distribution for water and waste management in Aurangabad.

SDG Goal: 11 | Target: 11.6 | Indicator: 11.6.1

Algorithms Used:

  • Heap Sort: Sorting algorithm for resource allocation. Application: Arranges resources efficiently for scheduling.
  • Radix Sort: Sorting algorithm for waste collection times or water distribution tasks. Application: Sorting waste collection or distribution tasks in a structured format.
  • Dijkstra's Algorithm: Computes the shortest path for water pipes and waste trucks. Application: Designing efficient routes for water and waste collection.
  • Floyd-Warshall Algorithm: Computes shortest paths for water distribution and waste collection. Application: Identifies optimal paths for resource management.
  • Greedy Algorithm for MST: Constructs a minimum spanning tree. Application: Designing optimal layouts for water and waste systems.
  • Ant Colony Optimization: Optimization inspired by ants finding the shortest path. Application: Optimizing water and waste collection routes.
