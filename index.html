What is the Course About?

The Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DSA) course equips students with essential knowledge and skills to solve computational problems efficiently through the design and analysis of algorithms. This course covers key algorithmic techniques, computational theory, and optimization strategies to enhance problem-solving abilities.

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Understand the fundamentals of algorithmic design.
  • Master key techniques such as Divide and Conquer, Dynamic Programming, and Greedy Algorithms.
  • Analyze time and space complexity using Big-O notation.
  • Design algorithms for sorting, searching, and graph traversal problems.
  • Implement and optimize algorithms to solve real-world problems.
  • Understand NP-Completeness and explore advanced optimization techniques.

Throughout the course, students will engage with hands-on coding exercises, participate in algorithmic challenges, and gain proficiency in implementing efficient solutions for real-world problems. By the end of the course, you will be able to evaluate algorithmic efficiency and choose the most appropriate algorithms for various problem types.
