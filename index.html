Welcome to the Design and Analysis of Algorithms Course

Embark on a journey through the fascinating world of algorithms, where we explore the principles that power the backbone of modern computing. This course is designed to equip you with the essential skills to analyze and develop efficient solutions for complex problems using algorithms and data structures.

Course Overview

Our course covers a comprehensive range of topics, including:

  • Fundamentals of Algorithms: Time and space complexities, recursion, and problem-solving techniques.
  • Advanced Data Structures: AVL Trees, Segment Trees, Red-Black Trees, and more.
  • Sorting and Searching Algorithms: Quick Sort, Heap Sort, Binary Search, and String Matching Algorithms.
  • Graph Algorithms: Dijkstra’s Shortest Path, Prim’s Minimum Spanning Tree, and Depth-First Search (DFS).
  • Optimization and Problem-Specific Algorithms: Union-Find, Knapsack Problem, and Traveling Salesperson Problem.

Real-World Applications

Learn how these algorithms apply to real-world challenges, such as:

  • Smart City Design: Optimizing infrastructure and traffic management.
  • Sorting and Searching: Efficiently processing large datasets in business and technology.
  • Tree Data Structures: Hierarchical data management in file systems and databases.
  • Competitive Programming: Leveraging algorithms to solve complex challenges in coding competitions.
  • Real-Time Decision Making: Optimization techniques for logistics, delivery, and system efficiency.
