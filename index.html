What is the Course About?

The Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DSA) course aims to equip students with the knowledge and skills needed to design and analyze algorithms to solve computational problems efficiently. This course provides a strong foundation in algorithmic techniques, problem-solving strategies, and the mathematical tools required to analyze algorithmic performance.

This course includes the following key topics:

  • Introduction to Algorithms: Learn what algorithms are, their importance, and how to approach algorithmic problem-solving.
  • Algorithm Design Techniques: Master basic design techniques like Brute Force, Greedy Algorithms, Divide and Conquer, Dynamic Programming, and Backtracking.
  • Time Complexity and Space Complexity: Understand Big-O notation, time and space complexity analysis, and how to compare the efficiency of different algorithms.
  • Sorting and Searching: Study classic algorithms like Bubble Sort, Merge Sort, Quick Sort, Binary Search, and their use cases.
  • Graph Algorithms: Dive into graph traversal methods like Depth First Search (DFS), Breadth First Search (BFS), and algorithms like Dijkstra's and Kruskal's for solving shortest path and minimum spanning tree problems.
  • Advanced Topics: Learn about NP-Complete problems, approximation algorithms, and techniques for optimization.

The course will be both theoretical and practical, allowing students to apply algorithmic techniques to real-world problems. You will be introduced to key concepts, learn to analyze algorithmic efficiency, and understand how to implement solutions to complex problems using the most efficient methods.

By the end of this course, you will be proficient in designing algorithms, analyzing their time and space complexities, and selecting the best algorithm for a given problem. You will also develop problem-solving skills that will help you tackle computational challenges across various domains.
