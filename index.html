What is the Course About?

Our course, Design and Analysis of Algorithms, introduces us to the fundamentals of algorithms and data structures, focusing on their design, analysis, and application.

We start with core concepts like time and space complexities, recursion, and backtracking. Next, we delve into tools like hashing and Union-Find, along with advanced data structures such as AVL Trees, Segment Trees, and Red-Black Trees. Sorting and searching algorithms, including Quick Sort, KMP, and Rabin-Karp, help us understand efficient data processing techniques.

Graph algorithms like Dijkstra’s shortest path and Prim’s spanning tree, and case studies such as the Travelling Salesperson Problem, show us how algorithms solve complex, real-world problems. The course also introduces concepts like NP-completeness and the limitations of algorithmic power.

By the end, we gain a strong foundation in computational thinking and the ability to develop efficient solutions to challenging problems.

What kind of data structures and algorithms have we studied so far?

Math Basics:
We started our journey to the algorithm world from understanding the basic concepts of maths which serve as fundamental for this course. In the math basics, we learnt how to find and calculate the time and space efficiency for a solution or recurrence relation. We also saw problems like the Tower of Brahma and the n-queens problem.

Math Foundations:
We then moved onto the math foundations where we were introduced to concepts like orders of growth. Using the order of growth, we understood in more detail about the efficiency of a solution.

Structured Data Management:
Further, we moved on to structured data management. In this, we came across n-ary trees, focusing on binary trees. We started with binary trees and advanced to binary search trees, AVL trees, Red-Black trees, and learned about heaps, tries, and skip lists.

How do you connect the course with real-time applications?

Many algorithms we study in this course draw inspiration from nature or real-world observations. For example, the shortest path algorithm mimics the work of electricians optimizing wiring paths. This course not only teaches the design of these algorithms but also offers insights into the thought processes behind their invention.

Real-World Applications:

  • Smart City Design: Algorithms like Dijkstra’s and Prim’s are used for optimizing road networks and transport systems.
  • Sorting and Searching: Sorting algorithms are crucial for organizing and processing large datasets.
  • Tree Data Structures: Used in file systems and compilers for efficient data management.
  • Hashing: Vital for cybersecurity, especially in password storage and data retrieval.
  • Competitive Programming: Techniques like dynamic programming and graph traversal are frequently used in coding competitions and real-world software development.
  • Optimization in Logistics: Algorithms like greedy and backtracking are used in logistics and real-time decision making.
